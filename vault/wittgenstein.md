# ludwig wittgenstein

![portrait photograph of ludwig wittgenstein, 1929](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Ludwig_Wittgenstein_1929.jpg)
*ludwig wittgenstein in 1929 — public domain, via [wikimedia commons](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Ludwig_Wittgenstein_1929.jpg)*

> **in one line:** wittgenstein wrote two incompatible philosophies of language — the early view that meaning is a fixed correspondence between words and facts, and the late view that meaning is nothing more than how words are actually used — and the second was a repudiation of the first.

## two philosophies, one philosopher

almost no one rewrites their own foundational work into its opposite. wittgenstein did. the *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (1921) and the *Philosophical Investigations* (1953) ask the same question — how does language have meaning? — and answer it incompatibly. the later work is a sustained critique of the assumptions underlying the earlier one.

### the early view — meaning as picture

the *Tractatus* advances the **picture theory of meaning**: a proposition is a logical picture of a possible state of affairs. language and world share a logical form; a sentence is meaningful exactly when it pictures a possible arrangement of things, and true when that arrangement actually obtains.

on this view, the boundary of meaningful language is sharp. whatever can be pictured can be said. whatever cannot be pictured — ethics, aesthetics, the meaning of life, God, the self — is not false but *unsayable*. it lies outside the bounds of sense; the right response to it is silence. wittgenstein thought this dissolved most philosophical problems by exposing them as attempts to say what can only be shown.

he also concluded that this solved philosophy, and largely stopped doing it.

### what broke the picture theory

he came back because the picture theory couldn't account for most of what language actually does. "slab!" shouted on a building site, a joke, a promise, a prayer, a greeting, a command — none of these *picture facts*, yet all are perfectly meaningful. meaning-as-correspondence-to-facts described one narrow corner of language and mistook it for the whole. the fix was not a patch; it required a different account of what meaning is.

### the late view — meaning as use

the *Philosophical Investigations* replaces the picture theory with a simpler, more radical claim: **the meaning of a word is its use in the language.** meaning is not a fixed correspondence established in advance; it is the actual behavior of the word in practice.

several ideas unfold from this:

**language-games.** language is not one unified system with a single grammar. it is a collection of distinct practices — giving orders, joking, greeting, praying, reporting, thanking, questioning — each with its own internal rules, embedded in ways of life. the word "game" connects these practices not because they share a definition but because they overlap and resemble one another in shifting, crisscrossing ways.

**family resemblance.** what do all games have in common? board games share rules and turn-taking; ball games share physical play; ring-a-ring-o'-roses shares neither. there is no single feature all games share. they are connected by a web of overlapping similarities, like the features shared across members of a family — no one trait runs through all of them. membership in a category can be a matter of resemblance rather than a shared essence.

**no private language.** wittgenstein argues that there cannot be a language in principle understandable only by one person, because meaning requires a **public standard of correctness** — something beyond your own say-so that separates using a word correctly from merely thinking you are. you cannot establish what a word means by a purely internal, private act. meaning is grounded in shared, checkable practice, not in individual intention.

**rule-following.** a rule does not contain its own application in advance. no formula fixes what counts as following it correctly in every future case. what makes "continue adding 2" come out as 1000, 1002, 1004 rather than something deviant is **a shared practice, a way of going on** — not a fact stored in the rule itself. rules get their force from the practices they are embedded in.

the overall movement is from meaning as a fixed, formal correspondence to meaning as something **emergent, public, and rooted in practice** — from the written contract to the living activity that gives words their life.

## where this account falls short

- **the break is real but not complete.** treating the *Tractatus* and *Investigations* as simply v1 and v2 overstates the discontinuity. scholars find significant continuities — the concern with the limits of language, the suspicion of misapplied metaphysics, the distinction between what can be said and what can only be shown. it is a substantial revision, not a complete reimplementation.
- **"meaning is use" is not itself a theory.** wittgenstein deliberately offers no systematic theory of meaning in the *Investigations* — he thought philosophy should describe uses and dissolve confusions, not build models. treating his view as a positive doctrine gets him backwards.
- **the picture theory retains its domain.** within its scope — formal logic, mathematics, the design of programming languages — the correspondence model is powerful and clarifying. it is not a refuted error but a scoped account with limits, still fully applicable within those limits.
- **the no-private-language argument is contested.** it is not a proof that isolated agents cannot have thoughts; it is a grammatical point about the conditions for *meaning* — a narrower claim than it is often taken to be.
- **language-games are not cleanly separated.** the picture of distinct, bounded language-games suggests tidier divisions than actually exist. joking bleeds into ordering, reporting bleeds into asserting. the family resemblances apply to language-games as much as to their content.
